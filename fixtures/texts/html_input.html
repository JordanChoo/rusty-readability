Understanding Readability

Readability formulas estimate how difficult a text is to read. They use measurements like sentence length, word length, and syllable counts to produce a score.

The most popular formula is the Flesch Reading Ease test. It was developed by Rudolf Flesch in 1948. A higher score means easier reading. Most consumer documents aim for a score between 60 and 70.

Other common formulas include the Gunning Fog Index, the Coleman-Liau Index, and the Automated Readability Index. Each formula emphasizes different aspects of text complexity.
